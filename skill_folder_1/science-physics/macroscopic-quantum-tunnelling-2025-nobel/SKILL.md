## Teaching Outline

### 1. The Surprise: Quantum Effects at Human Scales
- Quantum mechanics is usually associated with atoms, electrons, and photons.
- Tunnelling sounds impossible for something you can hold: a ball cannot pass through a solid wall.
- The 2025 Nobel laureates showed that a macroscopic circuit—about 1 cm across—can tunnel through an energy barrier as a single quantum object.

### 2. Superconductors and Cooper Pairs
- In normal conductors, electrons scatter and lose energy as heat: resistance.
- In a superconductor below a critical temperature, electrons form **Cooper pairs**.
- Cooper pairs are bound pairs with opposite spin and momentum; they act as a single quantum entity with a shared wavefunction.
- Billions of pairs act together rather than independently.

### 3. Josephson Junction
- Two superconductors separated by a very thin insulating barrier form a **Josephson junction**.
- The wavefunctions from each side overlap across the barrier.
- This arrangement allows purely quantum mechanical phenomena in an electrical circuit:
  - Supercurrent flows without voltage.
  - A voltage can appear when the system quantum-mechanically escapes its low-energy state.

### 4. What "Macroscopic Tunnelling" Means Here
- The circuit is biased with a weak current; it settles into a **zero-voltage metastable state**.
- Classically, that state should be stable because the current is too weak to overcome the barrier.
- Quantum mechanically, there is still a probability density for the system to be found across the barrier.
- Over many trials, the system occasionally **tunnels** out and produces a measurable voltage.
- The waiting time distribution behaves like a radioactive half-life, copying microscopic quantum decay statistics at a visible scale.

### 5. Quantised Energy Levels
- Microwaves of specific wavelengths are applied.
- The system absorbs energy only at particular wavelengths.
- That means its energy is not continuous but discretised: **quantised**.
- The wavelengths correspond to the spacing between superconducting energy levels, confirming quantum mechanics directly in the circuit.

### 6. Why This Is Foundationally Interesting
- Usually, macroscopic objects are either classical or made of many independent microscopic quantum parts.
- Here the entire macroscopic system has genuinely quantum behaviour, not just a statistical ensemble of microscopic effects.
- It is a clean experimental platform for testing quantum-to-classical boundaries, decoherence, and measurement theory.

### 7. Hardware Relevance
- Josephson junctions are the basis of **superconducting qubits** used in IBM, Google, Rigetti, and other quantum processors.
- Understanding macroscopic tunnelling and quantisation is essential for qubit readout, reset, and coherence management.
- The techniques demonstrated here underpin modern superconducting quantum computing architecture.

## Key Terms
- **Cooper pair**: paired superconducting electrons moving as a single quantum object.
- **Josephson junction**: two superconductors coupled across a thin insulator.
- **Metastable state**: a temporary equilibrium that can persist but is not the lowest-energy final state.
- **Quantum tunnelling**: crossing an energy barrier forbidden in classical mechanics.
- **Quantisation**: existence of only discrete allowed energy values.

## Educational Progression
- Start with the barrier-crossing intuition using a double-well metaphor.
- Introduce superconductivity and Cooper pairs as the prerequisite concept.
- Move to Josephson junction structure and circuit setup.
- Describe the experiment: zero-current trapping, tunnelling voltage events, microwave spectroscopy.
- End with implications for quantum computing hardware and foundational physics.
