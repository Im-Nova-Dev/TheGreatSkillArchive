## Entry Decision Rule
Enter this market if **any** of these are true:
1. You have RLHF, Constitutional AI, or red-teaming production experience.
2. You have published eval/filter work or safety benchmark results.
3. You can produce Responsible AI deliverables: model cards, data sheets, bias audit reports.

**Do not enter** if your only qualification is "I've used LLMs" — the premium is earned through demonstrated safety engineering, not general tool use.

## 90-Day Launch Sequence

### Days 1–7: Qualification Self-Test
1. Have I shipped an RLHF pipeline, safety filter, or Constitutional AI constraint in the last 6 months?
2. Can I produce a red-team report or eval report explaining methodology and findings?
3. Do I have a reference or public artifact (blog, paper, repo) showing safety/eval work?

If any answer is weak, stage first as a general AI implementation contractor, then build safety-specific proof.

### Days 8–21: Offer Design
Choose ONE:
- **AI Safety Retainer**: Weekly eval hygiene, red-team sprints, policy updates. $120–$180/hr or $6k–$12k/mo.
- **Compliance Readiness Sprint**: produce ISO 42001 / SOC 2 AI appendix, NIST AI RMF evidence map. $15k–$45k/fixed-fee.
- **Model Card & Governance Package**: model cards, data sheets, bias audit, approval workflow. $8k–$20k/module.

Write the offer in one sentence: *"I red-team and evaluate your production AI systems against jailbreaks, bias, and compliance frameworks so you can ship with audit-ready evidence."*

### Days 22–45: Pricing Validation
- Anchor off AI implementation rates: add **+15–25%** for safety-specific deliverables.
- Platform floors: Contra/Upwork ($65–$120/hr) → your target $115–$180/hr with explicit safety claims.
- If you have compliance work, charge enterprise retainer rates ($180–$260/hr) even at 5–10 hrs/mo.
- Recalibrate after 2 closes or 30 days of qualified contacts.

### Days 46–90: Outreach Operator
Target list:
1. Teams with public AI features but no "AI safety / Responsible AI" named role.
2. CFO / CTO teams at Series A/B startups preparing for SOC 2 / ISO 42001 (compliance buyer).
3. Enterprise AI product teams launching B2B features with enterprise security questionnaires.
4. AI consultancies that need subcontracted safety/eval capability.

Outreach format: *pain* → *proof* → *ask*.  
Example: *"Your new chatbot launched without jailbreak eval — a common gap. I've run 200+ red-team eval cycles for LLM products. Open to a 15-min scope?"*

## Compliance Calibration (Non-Negotiable)
- Your deliverable, especially for enterprise/government, should read like a policy artifact: structured eval report, signed guardrail agreement, model card.
- Keep separation between engineering compliance and AI safety; they're different line items.
- Maintain a red-team log template and an eval methodology doc that can be shared under NDA.
