## Entry Decision Rule
**Enter this market if you can credibly claim production review/audit experience
with AI-generated code, OR if you can architecture-review agentic systems.
Do not enter if your differentiator is “knowing AI tools” without production
review evidence.**

The market pays for trust and regression reduction, not tool familiarity.

## 90-Day Entry Sequence

### Days 1–7: Qualification Self-Test
Answer these without fluff:
1. Have I shipped or audited AI-generated code to production in the last 6 months?
2. Can I name 3 regression risks specific to agentic code that I have personally mitigated?
3. Do I have client/manager references for shipping speed + quality tradeoff decisions?

If any answer is weak, stage in implementation-only contracts first,
then convert to oversight after references.

### Days 8–21: Offer Design
Choose exactly one lead offer:
- **AI-Code QA Retainer**: architecture + PR review for AI-generated code.
  Weekly rhythm, 10–15 hrs, $5k–$10k/mo.
- **Agent-First Architecture Engagement**: agent task specs, guardrails,
  evaluation sets, review gates. Fixed-fee $8k–$25k per module.
- **Pilot-to-Production Integration**: end-to-end delivery from pilot to
  production. Fixed-fee $8k–$25k/module with monthly retainer upsell.

Write the offer in one sentence before composing longer copy.

### Days 22–45: Pricing Validation
- Pick a platform anchor (Arc, Toptal, Luminous, Lemon.io comparable).
- Set your rate at **+10–30% over platform floor** IF you have explicit
  oversight claims.
- If no oversight claims, match platform floor and sell implementation speed.
- Recalibrate after first 2 closes or 30 days of qualified contacts.

### Days 46–90: Outreach Operator
Target list (from intel and skill `selling-to-organizations-where-ai-displaces-junior-work`):
1. Teams publicly using AI coding agents without a named review lead.
2. Headcount-freeze teams with release commitments.
3. US-only hour overlap buyers in mid-market (Series A/B) who fear compliance exposure.
4. Government/contractor primes with large AI procurement mandates (2026 surge signal).

Outreach format: 3-line specific pain + 2-line proof + 1-line ask.
Do not pitch general “AI assistance”; pitch specific AI risk categories:
architectural drift, test coverage gaps, secrets leakage.

## Compliance Calibration (Non-Negotiable)
If you are targeting regulated industries (healthcare, finance, government):
- Hold oversight as the primary selling point, not implementation speed.
- Charge implementation/integration as a separate line item.
- Require signed guardrail agreements before beginning AI-agent code review work.
- Maintain a written audit trail template for each engagement.
