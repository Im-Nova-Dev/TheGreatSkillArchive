## Platform Rate Reality Checks

| Platform | Model | Fee Notes |

Competition signal: global platforms compress lower-bound rates because of worldwide competition; premium pricing requires production specialization, not general prompt skills.

## Geographic Rate Differential Strategy

North American senior AI Engineers currently command a +22% premium over European baseline. When remote, decide whether to:

1. compete on global rate and accept lower absolute income
2. specialize in high-premium verticals (healthcare AI, fintech, regulated industries)
3. leverage local market norms for in-person/hybrid premium

## Pricing Decision Checklist

Use this before quoting any AI contract or project:

- [ ] Does the work require production deployment or just prompt tuning?
- [ ] Is the ROI measurable in months (not years)?
- [ ] Is there regulated-vertical complexity (HIPAA, SOC 2, financial)?
- [ ] Does it require cross-industry domain knowledge?
- [ ] Have I benchmarked against current platform rate floors?

If the answer is "yes" to 2 or more questions, quote at least 20% above generalist AI floor.

## Common Pricing Mistakes

- **Bidding below $45/hr on AI work** — signals low trust and attracts low-quality clients. Start at $50+ for professional credibility.
- **Confusing output with outcomes** — clients evaluate AI engagements by cost savings and revenue lift, not API calls.
- **Ignoring geographic premium decay** — remote rate arbitrage is shrinking; North American premium is narrowing but still real (+22% for senior roles).
- **Not factoring churn** — AI contract durations average 9+ months; factor onboarding and handoff time into annualized rate planning.
