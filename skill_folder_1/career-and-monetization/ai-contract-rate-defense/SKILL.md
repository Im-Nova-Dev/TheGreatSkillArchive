# AI Contract Rate Defense
*Skill type: career-and-monetization*

## When to Use This Skill
- An AI automation/implementation client proposed a low hourly rate or project fee.
- You need a data-backed counteroffer, not generic "know your worth" advice.
- You want to convert hourly engagements into retainers or fixed-scope projects.

## 2. Pricing Logic: Why Rates Diverge

Rate variance reflects *what risk/value you absorb* for the client:

| Factor | Effect | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|
| You own integration risk | +15–30% | "I deliver working pipeline in prod, not a notebook." |
| You include evaluation + red-team testing | +10–20% | Deliverable includes eval script + failover runbooks. |
| You manage cross-system orchestration | +10–25% | Multiple APIs, DBs, auth providers in scope. |
| You commit to SLA or response window | +5–15% | Async + 2h weekly burst or on-call. |
| Client expects you to hire subs | +20–40% | Fractional lead rate vs. individual contributor. |
| Cross-border/tax complexity | +5–10% | You handle multi-jurisdiction invoicing and compliance. |

**Rule:** If you are not transferring risk, you should not expect premium pricing.

## 3. Anchoring & Objection Handling Cheat Sheet

Use these verbatim-style responses when the client pushes for lower fees:

| Client Objection | Counter Response |
|------------------|------------------|
| "Your rate is above our budget." | "Happy to adjust scope to fit budget. What business outcome is most important to protect? We can stage delivery so you get value now and expand later." |
| "Can you do $X/hr?" | "That range is aligned to staff-augmentation, not implementation ownership. I operate project-level with outcome guarantees. We can split into a scoped pilot first to prove ROI, then revisit for full deployment." |
| "Others quoted lower." | "Understand. If you want lowest cost, staff augmentation vs. implementation lead is a different service class. For production behavior and not another pilot, I structure fixed-scope deliverables so you know exact cost and timeline." |
| "We just need someone prompt-engineer our app." | "Prompt tuning is part one; production eval, monitoring, and fallback behavior are what keep the system from failing in front of customers. My retainer includes all of it." |
| "Can you commit 40 hrs/week?" | "I prefer project/retainer models because they give you clearer milestones and lower risk of patchwork. If you need embedded team time, my fractional lead rate is $X/mo." |

## 4. Structuring for Higher Fees

### From Hourly → Fixed Project
1. Deliverable: "One working AI workflow deployed + eval package + handoff runbook."
2. Price: 2–3× estimated hours × your hourly floor.
3. Payment terms: 50% upfront, 25% at integration demo, 25% at handoff.

### From Hourly → Retainer
1. Scope: weekly integrations, evals, monitoring, sprint prep, and on-call review window.
2. Price: floor at $3.5k/mo for indie SMB; $7k+ for mid-market; $12k+ for enterprise.
3. Term: monthly with 30-day exit; most clients stay 3+ months if you show metrics.

### From Hourly → Fractional Lead / AI Implementation Lead
1. Scope: run implementations, interface with stakeholders, hire subs, own roadmap.
2. Price: $6k–$12k/mo depending on team size and client revenue band.
3. Guardrail: explicit cap on hours or billable ops work to prevent scope creep.

## 7. Split-Test Script for First Calls

Use this 60-second sequence to lock higher rates on discovery calls:

1. Define outcome: "If we succeed, what will be true in 90 days that isn't true today?"
2. Quantify impact: "Is that worth $X to you in time, revenue, or risk reduction?"
3. Present options: "Project fee for scoped delivery is $X; retainer for ongoing iteration is $Y/mo. Which aligns to how you want to measure success?"

This converts buyers from "how many hours?" to "what does this solve?" and pulls the conversation toward value, not cost.
