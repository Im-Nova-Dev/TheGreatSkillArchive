# AI Displacement Positioning & Monetization — Evidence Playbook

## When to Use
- Setting or defending freelance/contract rates in 2025–2026
- Choosing a niche: AI integration vs generalist dev work
- Evaluating open-source sponsorship or bounty paths
- Translating market signals into proposals and positioning statements
- Advising a technical professional on pricing or niche selection

## Core Rules
- Favor exact quantities, named sources, and recent data over generic advice.
- Use the player's own stack and past rates as anchors; do not invent numbers.
- Separate short-term pricing tactics from multi-quarter positioning strategy.
- When citing research, use official organization names and report editions, not blog summaries.

## Intel Anchors (2025)
### AI Role Pricing Premium
- **Source:** Ravio 2025 Tech Job Market Report (1,700+ companies; global total-rewards surveys).
- **Signal:** Job titles containing "AI" rose from **0.32% of all tech roles in 2024** to **2.17% in early 2025** (~7x jump).
- **Pricing implication:** AI-focused software engineering roles command new-hire salary premiums. Contractors with delivered AI/ML work can cite reduced hiring competition and higher budget allocations.

### Entry-Level Collapse
- **Source:** Same Ravio dataset.
- **Signal:** Entry-level hiring across tech **collapsed by 73.4% in 2025**; junior roles in People, Marketing, and Engineering fell **75–84% YoY**.
- **Pricing implication:** Clients expect senior-for-cheaper mixes; resist by specializing in high-value delivery (AI integration, observability, security) rather than competing on hourly rate.

### Admin/Support Roles Under Pressure
- Scrum Master hiring steeply declining; repeatable admin tasks being AI-automated.
- **Pricing implication:** Scrum Master or PM-style contractors must rebrand around outcome ownership, release rhythm, or platform reliability.

### Remote Work Dominance Continues
- **Source:** Landing.Jobs 2025 Tech Talent Trends Report (1,516 Portugal-resident tech professionals surveyed Dec 2024–Mar 2025).
- **Signal:** Full remote roles grew **3.3% YoY to 50%**, 3rd consecutive year of growth.
- **Signal:** Contractors are **4.5x more likely** to work cross-border remotely than full-time employees.
- **Pricing implication:** Remote-first positioning is standard; advanced positioning is cross-border English fluency and async delivery competence.

### Open-Source Sponsorship Reality
#### MapLibre Q4 2025 (Oct 1 – Dec 31, 2025)
- MapLibre Sponsorship Program: **$85,000 total**, including **$75,000 from AWS**, **$10,000 from CalTopo**.
- Maintenance spending: **$41,678** from general fund (web $26,488; native $15,190).
- Developer bounty in Q4: **$1,000** from Development Fund.
- Key lesson: Enterprises absorb large sponsorship to ensure reliability; maintainers still underfunded on maintenance heavy lifting.

#### PHP Foundation 2025
- Total 2025 contributions: **$730,534**.
- 11 contracted core developers funded; 536 sponsors/individual contributors.
- Founded major deliverables: PHP 8.5, PIE 1.0, PHP SDK for MCP.
- Key lesson: Sustained contributor pools require year-long donor commitments, not ad-hoc bounties.

## Positioning Templates (Evidence-Based)

### Positioning for AI Integration Contracting
Use this structure when quoting or writing proposals:

> "I build [specific stack] integrations that use AI to [reduce manual work / increase conversion / cut support tickets]. Last month I implemented [concrete result]. Based on 2025 market data, AI-focused engineering roles have jumped 0.32% → 2.17% of all tech postings, while entry-level hiring has dropped 73.4%. Most teams cannot hire AI talent fast enough; my engagement de-risks that gap with shipped outcomes."

### Positioning for Cross-Border Remote
> "I work full remote with async delivery as my default. In 2025 full-remote tech roles grew 3.3% year-over-year to 50%, and contractors are 4.5x more likely than employees to work cross-border remotely. I am English-fluent with shipping documentation and tests asynchronously."

### Positioning for Open-Source Sponsorship
When approaching enterprise sponsors:

> "MapLibre-style sponsorships show that organizations will commit $75k+ when your library is infrastructure-grade. I maintain [project] which solves [problem statement]. If you rely on this in production, I offer [maintenance tier / priority SLA / shipped feature] to reduce your maintenance tax."

## Niche Selection Signals
Good 2025–2026 niches based on evidence:
1. **AI integration / automation contracts** — premium pricing, fast demand growth.
2. **Platform reliability, observability, and security** — hiring decline in admin roles increases outsourcing demand.
3. **Enterprise-backed open-source maintainer** — viable if stack has infrastructure role and you can keep AWS/GCP/Azure sponsorship relationships warm.
4. **Cross-border remote senior contracting** — highest uptake in places like Portugal and emerging EU tech hubs; set USD/EUR rates anchored by local senior band + remote premium.

## Pricing Logic
### Anchor points to reference in proposals
- Fullstack/backend location: survey real market data rather than stating a generic rate.
- AI/ML contractor premium: reference Ravio hiring surge + reduced candidate competition.
- Rate increase justification: use "I am delivering outcomes that teams cannot hire for internally under current entry-level hiring collapse."

### Floor raising tactic
- ask for a fixed project fee tied to measurable outcome rather than hourly work whenever possible.

## Verification Steps
1. Cross-check every rate/yearly claim with the report cited above before sending.
2. Confirm any "7x" or "73.4%" numbers and attribute them correctly.
3. When referencing GitHub Sponsors or Open Collective figures, cite real quarterly reports like MapLibre Q4 2025.

## Common Pitfalls
- Using vague "AI is hot" claims without numbers.
- Positioning as "cheap senior" when pricing under entry-level workers collapses your floor.
- Seeking OSS funding without calculating maintenance cost vs sponsorship revenue; MapLibre's $41k+/quarter maintenance outpaces individual sponsorships.
- Over-trusting Indeed/Hacker News vibes; use named report editions and surveyed populations.
