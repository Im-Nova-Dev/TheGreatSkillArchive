# AI Dev Tools Market Demand Teaching

## Teaching step 1 — Diagnose market pressure
- Share one concrete market signal per session: analyst spend tracker, job-board keyword growth, platform funding.
- Player names often matter more than percentages for building credibility.
- Compare demand in "new build" vs "modernization + AI enablement" segments.

## Teaching step 2 — Select one high-signal niche
Recommended starting niches:
1. Legacy-to-agent migration (Java/TS monoliths to agent-assisted flows)
2. AI coding agent audit + red-teaming for enterprise codebases
3. Developer productivity engineering (ADOP + AI toolchains)

Criteria: repeatable service, clear before/after metric, deliverable in 2–4 weeks.

## Teaching step 3 — Build a repeatable offer template
- Name the outcome, not the task (e.g., "AI onboarding sprint" vs "AI consulting").
- Bundle 3 layers: discovery, implementation, runbook.
- Price anchoring: use day rates or fixed sprints; avoid hourly to prevent scope bleeding.

## Teaching step 4 — Create demand evidence material
- 5-slide deck: problem → market signal → named players → case-shape → outcome metric.
- One-page cheat sheet for buyers: "Why now / Why you / Proof / Scope / Next step".

## Teaching step 5 — Practice one conversation
- Cold outreach sequence: 4 messages, no links, no fluff.
- Discovery call script: 3 symptom questions, 1 consequence question, 1 next-step ask.
- Objection handling for "we're waiting for budget" and "we have internal capability".

## Pitfalls to avoid
- Don't lead with AI hype; lead with migration debt, review cost, or release-cycle risk.
- Avoid "we do everything AI"; narrower claims sell faster.
- Don't chase FOMO trends older than 6 months without linking them to a paying workflow.
