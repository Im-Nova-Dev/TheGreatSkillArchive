# OSS Security Research + AI Contract Adjacent-Domain Playbook

## Purpose
Convert under-tapped adjacent domains — web3/OSS security research and AI implementation contracting for agent-displaced orgs — into prioritized, evidence-backed contract choices when core AI engineering is saturated, priced down, or seasonally weak.

## When to Use This Playbook
- After updating career intel and noticing AI implementation rates are compressing or demand is shifting from implementers to governance/oversight.
- When an engineer wants to enter security/OSS bounty research without burning weeks on lookback-only learning.
- When the strongest signal points away from traditional "software engineer" roles toward hybrid, contract, or fractional work.

## Adjacent Domain 1: OSS / Web3 Security Research

### Evidence Base (from intel store)
- Immunefi absorbed Code4rena in 2026; former auditors need a new funnel.
- April 2026 alone had ~30 major DeFi exploit incidents.
- Immunefi payout ceilings reach $10M for critical vulnerabilities.
- Opire serves as a micro-bounty layer for non-web3 OSS.
- Active researchers report transitioning from Code4rena to full-time independent research.

### Target Niches
1. DeFi protocol audits on Immunefi.
2. Bridge/cross-chain security research.
3. Non-web3 OSS bug/feature bounties via Opire.

### Transition Thresholds
- Minimum before billing: 3 completed public audits or 5 closed bounties.
- Start paired research (senior + you) at 40–60% of senior rate until solo qualifying audits exist.
- Retainer shape: (audit pipeline) + (advisory) — pure bug hunt is the marketing funnel; advisory is the margin.

### Pricing Dereference
- Solo bug bounty: $200–$5,000 for mid-severity findings.
- Advisory retainer: $3k–$10k/month per protocol.
- Warm lead path: project Discord, security Twitter, Immunefi research newsletter.

### Time-Budget Rule
- Maximum 20% of billable time on lookback/learning from a generalist base.
- One closed audit is worth 50 blog posts for conversion.
- Avoid pure CTF research without real protocol exposure.

## Adjacent Domain 2: AI Implementation Contracting (Agent Displacement Play)

### Evidence Base (from intel store)
- 50–55% of US jobs will be reshaped by AI in the next 2–3 years (BCG, 2026).
- 60%+ of entry-level tech roles now require 3+ years of experience (experience paradox).
- Enterprise AI coding agent adoption is now an internal mandate, raising bug density.
- Legacy monoliths are the largest barrier to AI agent embedding (FirstLine Software, 2026).
- Developer reports from H1 2026: junior-level work is being automated poorly, creating cleanup/review demand.

### Target Niches
1. AI coding agent oversight + code review retainer (guardrails for Copilot/Cursor/Codex-internal).
2. Agent workflow bridge engineering (middleware connecting LLM agents to legacy web apps without APIs).
3. RAG/agentic pipeline last-mile integration.
4. AI readiness diagnostics for engineering-led companies.

### Pricing Dereference
- Hourly floor: $100–$200/hr for implementation + oversight.
- Fixed-fee PoC: $5k–$20k depending on scope.
- Retainer: $2k–$8k/month for ongoing code review and guardrail maintenance.

### Positioning Copy Template
> "I implement and govern AI development tooling for engineering teams whose productivity gains are being undermined by junior-output errors, legacy-code blockage, and agent hallucination risk."

### Contract Acquisition Step Sequence
1. Identify companies that recently announced AI acceleration in press or job postings.
2. Qualify by checking for open AI-related roles with no dedicated AI ops/implementation hire.
3. Pitch an outcome-shaped scope: "30-day AI rollout readiness with migration roadmap."
4. Anchor fixed-fee, not hourly; convert high-value engagements to monthly oversight retainers.
5. Collect testimonials focused on defect-rate reduction and feature-ship velocity.

### Time-Budget Rule
- Maximum 30% of billable time spent on custom PoC demos.
- Reuse cross-domain assets: a browser-automation POC for one client becomes a template for another.
- Overservice the first 60 days to secure 6-month renewals.

## Cross-Domain Decision Matrix

| Factor | OSS/Web3 Security Research | AI Implementation Contracting |
|---|---|---|
| Learning curve | Steep | Moderate |
| Seasonality | Tied to exploit cycles | Tied to enterprise budget cycles |
| Geographic arbitrage | Moderate | High |
| Lookback cost | Medium | Low–Medium |
| Contract renewal | Ad hoc (per audit/bounty) | Recurring (review/oversight retainers) |
| Risk | Exploit-discovery reputational | Integration work reputational |
| Best for profile | Engineers with CTF/formal verification interest | Engineers comfortable with messy enterprise code |
