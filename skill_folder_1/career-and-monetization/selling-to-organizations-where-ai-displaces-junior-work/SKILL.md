# Selling to Organizations Where AI Displaces Junior Work

When AI coding agents ship first drafts, repeatable implementation is
commoditized. The premium moves to humans who set task boundaries,
audit outputs, and own system-level integrity.

## When to Use

- You see buyers using AI agents/Copilots but expressing concern about regressions,
  inconsistent quality, or architectural drift.
- You want to replace some fraction of junior dev capacity with retained,
  higher-cost AI-supervision work.

## Buyer Signals to Target

- Mentions of Claude Code, Jules, Codex CLI, Copilot Workspace in public
  repos, job posts, or LinkedIn commentary.
- Teams advertising "AI-first" or "agent-native" engineering, especially
  when they do not list a dedicated code-review lead.
- Mid-market companies that have cut headcount but still need releases.
- EU/US hybrid buyers who need US-hour overlap and are comfortable remote.

## Offer Design

1. **AI-Code QA Retainer**
   - Weekly architecture review + PR review for AI-generated code
   - Risk: architectural drift, test coverage gaps, secrets leakage
   - Default deal: $5k–$10k/mo retainer, 10–15 billed hours/week

2. **Agent-First Architecture Engagement**
   - Define agent task specs, guardrails, evaluation sets
   - Design review gates and escalation triggers
   - Fixed-fee $8k–$25k per domain/module

3. **Hybrid Delivery Model**
   - You direct AI agents; junior contractors execute
   - Your premium is classifying "AI-manageable" vs. "needs human" scope

## Positioning Language That Converts

- "I ship with Claude Code at full speed, then review for
  production-grade results."
- "I treat AI code as generated scaffolding, not finished work."
- "I replace 1.5–2.0 junior FTE per month at lower cost and higher
  release confidence."

## Pricing

- Senior AI-architecture review: $180–$260/hr
- Full implementation + oversight: $130–$190/hr
- Fractional CTO for agentic stack: $140–$200/hr
- Retainer minimums: $3k–$8k/mo for < 18h/week
- For comparison: 2026 AI Engineer median is ~$161k/yr and AI
  Consultant ~$125k/yr; monthly equivalent of $10k+ is competitive
  for the oversight/value offered.

## Outreach Play

1. Build one-page case study: before/after metrics (releases per month,
   bug escape rate) with AI supervision.
2. DM CTOs/VP Eng at companies that just raised Series A/B, especially
   those that mention AI adoption.
3. Use January–June budget windows when engineering leaders are planning
   headcount and contractors are first-line additions.

## Anti-Patterns

- Selling "prompt engineering" only; that is a commodity.
- Competing on price with AI agents without adding review/governance.
- Chasing firms with clear low-risk, repeatable implementation work;
  those buyers will cut your contract once AI confidence rises.
