# Molecular Hydrogen Superfluidity — Intel Summary

**Source**: *Science Advances*, DOI 10.1126/sciadv.adu1093, February 2025  
**Team**: Hatsuki Otani, Susumu Kuma, Shinichi Miura, Majd Mustafa, Jeff C. W. Lee, Pavle Djuricanin, Takamasa Momose (UBC, RIKEN, Kanazawa University)

---

## Core Discovery (Condensed)

First experimental observation of superfluidity in **any molecule** — parahydrogen (p-H₂) clusters in helium nanodroplets at 0.4 K.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Temperature | 0.4 K (helium droplet bath) |
| Probe | Methane (CH₄) ν₄ bending mode, 65 MHz linewidth |
| Observable | Sticking factor κ (fraction of H₂ shell co-rotating with methane) |
| Superfluid threshold | N > 10 p-H₂ molecules per cluster |
| Bosonic exchange fraction | >60% for N ≥ 5 |
| Timeline | ~20 years to achieve |

---

## Why Bulk H₂ Failed

- H₂ freezing point: **13.8 K**
- Predicted superfluid transition: **1–2 K** (Ginzburg 1972)
- Bulk H₂ solidifies *before* reaching superfluid Tc

**Escape route**: Finite-size clusters in helium nanodroplets remain liquid-like down to 0.4 K.

---

## Measurement Principle: "Miniature Andronikashvili Experiment"

| Andronikashvili (1946) | Momose et al. (2025) |
|------------------------|----------------------|
| Rotating disks in bulk ⁴He | Spinning methane molecule in p-H₂ cluster |
| Measures normal fluid fraction via disk deceleration | Measures sticking factor κ via rotational spectroscopy |
| Macroscopic rotor | Single-molecule nanoscale rotor |

**κ = 1** → rigid coupling (normal fluid)  
**κ → 0** → perfect slippage (superfluid)

---

## Cluster-Size Evolution (Key Teaching Data)

| Cluster size (N) | κ behavior | Physical state |
|------------------|------------|----------------|
| N < 6 | κ ≈ 1 | Normal fluid — friction dominates |
| N ≈ 10 | κ drops sharply | Onset of superfluidity |
| N > 10 | κ → 0 | Perfect superfluid — collective quantum entity |

Path-integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) simulations match the κ(N) curve quantitatively.

---

## Physical Mechanism

p-H₂ = **composite boson** (total nuclear spin I = 0)  
→ Bose-Einstein condensation of center-of-mass motion  
→ Same fundamental physics as ⁴He superfluidity

**Differences from ⁴He**:
- Lighter mass
- **Molecular** (internal rotational/vibrational DOF)
- Predicted Tc **higher** than ⁴He (1–2 K vs 2.17 K)

## Future Directions (from paper)

1. **Size evolution**: N = 20 → >10⁶ molecules; map quantum-to-classical boundary
2. **External fields**: Electric/magnetic response to probe quantum behaviors
3. **Applications**: Clean-energy hydrogen storage/transport; frictionless flow insights

## Significance Statement

- Resolves 50+ year debate (Ginzburg 1972 prediction)
- First molecular superfluid — new testbed beyond helium/atomic gases
- Finite-size platform to watch superfluidity *emerge* molecule by molecule
- Clean-energy relevance: quantum properties of hydrogen under confinement
