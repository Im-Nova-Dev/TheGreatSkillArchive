# Ambient-Pressure Nickelate Superconductivity

Use this skill when asked why bilayer nickelate thin films can superconduct at ambient pressure while bulk La3Ni2O7 needs >14 GPa, how epitaxial strain, ozone annealing, and cation substitution tune Tc, and what first principles govern the pairing symmetry, dimensionality, and device relevance.

## Teaching order
1. Start with normal conductivity versus superconductivity as a macroscopic quantum state.
2. Introduce cuprates as the clue map and nickelates as the nearest neighbor analog.
3. Explain Ruddlesden-Popper layering and why bilayer matters.
4. Derive why pressure was originally required: structural phase versus lattice geometry.
5. Explain epitaxial strain as a solid-state lever and ozone annealing as a chemical lever.
6. Survey thin-film results, Tc tables, and what the ARPES debates mean.
7. End with device implications and realistic steps toward engineering applications.

## 1. What is superconductivity, generally
A superconducting material carries electric current with **zero DC resistance** and expels magnetic flux via the **Meissner effect**. Both behaviors arise from a macroscopic quantum phase where electrons form **Cooper pairs**—bound pairs of electrons that condense into the same lowest-energy quantum state. The paired state has an energy gap; breaking a pair costs at least the superconducting gap energy, which is why a DC dissipationless current can flow.

- Superconducting transition temperature Tc

## 2. Cuprates are the clue map
High-Tc superconductivity was discovered in cuprates in 1986. The key structural features:
- Copper-oxygen planes
- Apical oxygen above/below each Cu site
- Doping away from half-filling changes copper valence

Nickel is one column to the right of copper in the periodic table, with analogous chemistry, so infinite-layer nickelates were predicted to have similar electronic physics.

## 3. Ruddlesden-Popper phases and bilayer geometry
The Ruddlesden-Popper (RP) series stacks n octahedral NiO2 planes separated by rock-salt LaO layers:
- n = 1: single-layer LaNiO3-like
- n = 2: bilayer La3Ni2O7
- n = 3: trilayer La4Ni3O10
- n = ∞: infinite-layer NdNiO2-type
