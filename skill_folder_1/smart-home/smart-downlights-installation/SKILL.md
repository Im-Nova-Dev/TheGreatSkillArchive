# Smart Recessed Downlights Installation Mastery

## Core concept
Smart downlights replace or sit inside recessed “can” housings. Two product paths: (1) retrofit kits for existing cans, (2) new-construction slim panels.

## Retrofit vs new construction
  - Retrofit kit: spring clips or twist-lock base that grabs inside an existing 4in or 6in can.
  - New construction: LED panel sits in TRIM or housing from below; needs rough-in frame.
  - J-box vs can: some direct-wire into standard octagon/round boxes.

## Pre-installation checks
- Measure can interior diameter. Most common: 4”, 5”, 6”. Import-tolerant kits use 5-6in adapter clips.
- Verify max fixture height in box: some slim panels are <2in tall, but standard “high-lumen” downlights are deeper.
- Check insulation contact rating: IC-rated for direct contact with blown-in cellulose; NON-IC for open attic plenum.
- Circuit amperage: do not overload 15A circuit with >80% rule. Most LED downlights are 10-18W; 20 of them still fits on one circuit.

## Wiring safety
- Turn breaker off at panel; confirm dead with non-contact tester.
- Disconnect existing fixture wires. Match line/hot, neutral, ground.
- Some “smart driver” downlights have separate driver box in attic; connect driver first, then LED to driver low-voltage leads.
- Retrofit with E26 base: treat like ordinary bulb replacement, no wiring.

## Installation steps
1. Remove old trim + lamp (if present).
2. Disconnect old fixture from house wiring.
3. For new dropdown: attach mounting frame to joist or hanger bar.
4. Run NM-B 12/2 or 14/2 from switch leg to junction box inside can.
5. Connect hot, neutral, ground.
6. Install gasket/seal can for air-seal (required in many jurisdictions).
7. Insert LED downlight into trim; twist or spring-lock.
8. Restore power; app discovery follows.

## Trim and ceiling finish
- Baffle trim: reduces glare; use in living areas.
- Reflector trim: maximizes lumens; use in high ceilings or garages.
- Gimbal trim: adjustable aim; good for wall-wash or art lighting.

## Smart-specific issues
- Hue Recessed Downlight: behaves like a Hue bulb after pairing. Pair before final trim install so app can confirm state.
- Dimming curves: Lutron Caseta + Hue downlight may behave oddly. Prefer Hue switches/dimmers or keep downlight at 100% with scenes.
- Heat: enclosed IC-rated housings trap heat. Use IC-rated LEDs only; never exceed rated temp.

## Troubleshooting
  Symptom                           | Cause                           | Fix
  ----------------------------------|---------------------------------|---------------------------------------
  Light flickers on/off rapidly      | Loose line/neutral               | Tighten wire nuts; resolder
  Downlight won’t pair              | Already paired to another bridge | Factory reset per vendor instructions
  Half bright / color casting        | Wrong CCT selected                | Re-pick white point in app
  Humming                            | Incompatible trailing-edge dimmer | Replace switch with trailing-edge or remove dimmer

## Teaching drills
- Drill 1: apprentice measures existing can size and selects correct retrofit adapter without looking at spec sheet.
- Drill 2: live demo using training can; apprentice completes removal + install in <15 min while narrating safety steps.
- Drill 3: compare IC vs NON-IC installation plans on paper.
