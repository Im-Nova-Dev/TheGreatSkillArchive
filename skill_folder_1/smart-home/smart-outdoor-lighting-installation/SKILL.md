# Smart Outdoor Lighting Installation Mastery

## Why outdoors need different treatment
Garden/outdoor smart lighting deals with moisture, IR, long cable runs, UV plastics, and critters. Rules from indoor install often fail outside.

## Outdoor category vocabulary
  - Path / bollard: short post or stake lights; low voltage.
  - Spot / flood: directional; usually mounted to house or posts.
  - Landscape wash: wide-angle wash lighting for grass beds.
  - String / festoon: overhead between posts / trees.
  - Strips: under eaves, step lighting, deck rail lighting.

## Power topologies
  - Low-voltage landscape system: transformer steps 120V to 12V AC or DC; cable runs to fixtures. Keep runs < 100ft on 12/2 landscape cable.
  - Mains outdoor-rated: Hue Calla, Govee Outdoor Spot 2; hardwired into junction boxes outside.
  - Solar + smart hub retrofit: some paths integrate smart plug into transformer; cheaper but dependent on sun.

## IP rating rules
  - IP44 = splashing water okay; fine under eaves or covered patio.
  - IP65 = jet-resistant; near sprinklers okay but not submersion.
  - IP67 = submersion 1m; install near ground or in-ground bollards.
  - Wrong IP = corrosion inside sealed fixture; warranty usually voided.

## Installation pre-requisites
- Call before digging: 811 in US. Landscaper-grade cable often crosses property at unknown depth.
- Wire gauge: 12AWG for runs up to 100ft at transformer; 10AWG beyond. Thinner wire = voltage drop + dim far lights.
- GFCI: all outdoor outlets and low-voltage transformer secondary circuits need GFCI protection. Use GFCI breaker or adapter.
- Wire connections outside: use gel-filled heat-shrink connectors or waterproof wire nuts + silicone. Do not use electrical tape alone.

## Installing low-voltage bollard/path lights
1. Mount transformer near outlet; do not bury transformer. Above-grade, ventilated, within 50ft of first fixture.
2. Lay cable on grade, use lawn staples every 36 inches.
3. Fixture mounting:
   - Stake lights: dig with rod or spade to prescribed depth, insert spike, backfill tamp.
   - Hardwired bollard: install in concrete with J-box (some locale code requires conduit sweep).
4. Connect fixture leads to main cable using gel connector; orient + to +, - to -.
5. At transformer: connect secondary leads, set voltage (if adjustable; 12V or 15V per fixture load).
6. Restore power; remote or app discovery.

## Hardwired mains spots/Hue outdoor
1. Shut off breaker. Verify dead.
2. Install j-box or surface-mount rated box on siding/post.
3. Run UF-B 12/2 cable from switch/transformer to box.
4. Connect hot/neutral/ground; fixture’s built-in driver accepts line voltage.
5. Mount fixture with included bracket; aim before final tighten.
6. Restore service; pair with Hue Bridge inside.

## Motion sensor placement outdoors
- Height 6-8ft. Angle 45° downward. Avoid mounted near heat sources (AC compressor, dryer vent) to reduce false positives.
- Range: Hue Outdoor Motion ~39ft; place to cover walkways without sensing street.
- Use lux value: set minimum lux threshold in app so porch light doesn’t fire at noon.

## Wiring pitfalls
  Pitfall                              | Symptom                          | Fix
  -------------------------------------|----------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------
  Reversed +/- on low voltage           | Flicker, half brightness          | Swap leads at transformer or fixture
  No GFCI trip training                | Real fault not protected          | Press TEST monthly on outdoor GFCI devices
  Cable buried <6in                     | Nicked by shovel/earth           | Bury >=6in marked cable or run in conduit
  Transformer undersized                | Dim far lights                    | Add second transformer or upgrade VA rating

## Teaching drills
- Drill 1: Translation exercise. Apprentice converts between “100ft @ 12V” load, cable gauge, and transformer size.
- Drill 2: Connector lab. Use gel connectors on scrap cable; apprentice completes 6 connections, each must pass continuity + water pressure test simulation.
- Drill 3: Magnetic aiming. Set two spotlights, apprentice sets scene angles and validates in app without crawling on ladder again.

## Post-install
- [ ] All connections waterproofed and mechanically secured.
- [ ] GFCI tested.
- [ ] App discovery and motion/light triggers configured.
- [ ] Schedule set (sunset to sunrise, or specific hours).
- [ ] Weatherproof covers closed and gland tightened.
- [ ] Cable marked on landscape plan at 1ft depth minimum.
